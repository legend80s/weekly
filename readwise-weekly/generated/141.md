## 一、Most highlighted Articles of the week

### 1.1 [Do I belong in tech anymore?](https://ky.fyi/posts/ai-burnout/)

![Do I belong in tech anymore?](https://readwise-assets.s3.amazonaws.com/media/wisereads/articles/do-i-belong-in-tech-anymore/cover_image_uzth0zS.png)

Design engineer Ky Decker quit their well-paying remote job. He reflects on leaving tech after AI-driven burnout, and on what gets lost when speed becomes the priority. "The point of a code review is not simply for good code to make it into a codebase, but to build institutional knowledge as people debate and iterate and compromise, slow as it may be. Friction is good."

### 1.2 [A.I. Should Elevate Your Thinking, Not Replace It](https://koshyjohn.com/blog/ai-should-elevate-your-thinking-not-replace-it/)

![A.I. Should Elevate Your Thinking, Not Replace It](https://readwise-assets.s3.amazonaws.com/media/wisereads/articles/a-i-should-elevate-your-thinki/cover_image_WIuKCxO.jpg)

There's a difference between using AI as leverage and using AI as a crutch, says Microsoft's Koshy John, and the line is easy to miss. "You can outsource mechanics, accelerate research and compress routine tasks. You can remove enormous amounts of low-value labor. All of that is good and should happen. But you cannot skip the formation of skill and expect to possess it anyway."

Koshy John · 6 mins | **koshyjohn.com**
### 1.3 [BEWARE SOFTWARE BRAIN](https://theverge.com/podcast/917029/software-brain-ai-backlash-databases-automation/)

![BEWARE SOFTWARE BRAIN](https://readwise-assets.s3.amazonaws.com/media/wisereads/articles/beware-software-brain/cover_image_L2VvC5r.jpg)

Nilay Patel, co-founder of The Verge, has a name for the worldview driving tech's disconnect with the public: software brain. "Not everything is a business, not everything is a loop, and the entire human experience cannot be captured in a database. That’s the limit of software brain. That’s why people hate AI. It flattens them. Regular people don’t see the opportunity to write code as an opportunity at all. The people do not yearn for automation."

Nilay Patel · 13 mins | **The Verge**

## 二、Most highlighted YouTube Video of the week

### [Stanford Neuroscientist: Can't Remember Your Dreams? Your Brain May Be Warning You!](https://youtube.com/watch/?v=m-nnyNZ0TQ0)

![Stanford Neuroscientist: Can't Remember Your Dreams? Your Brain May Be Warning You!](https://readwise-assets.s3.amazonaws.com/media/wisereads/articles/stanford-neuroscientist-can-t-/cover_image.jpg)

Neuroscientist David Eagleman, author of Livewired, shares his lab's striking theory about why humans dream. "The purpose of dreaming is to defend the visual territory from takeover from the other senses. Because we live on a planet that rotates into darkness for half the time, the visual cortex is at a disadvantage. So every 90 minutes you've got this very ancient thing in your midbrain that shoots random activity into the visual system, and only the visual system."

The Diary Of A CEO

## 五、Hand-picked book of the week

### [Invisible Influence](https://readwise.io/reader/fd/450748809)

![Invisible Influence](https://readwise-assets.s3.amazonaws.com/media/wisereads/articles/invisible-influence/cover_image_HpkE008.jpg)

You picked your car rationally: best mileage, safest, the right fit. Your neighbor who bought the same one? They were swayed by other people. Obviously. In Invisible Influence, Wharton marketing professor Jonah Berger, bestselling author of Contagious and The Catalyst, has bad news. Social influence shapes nearly every choice we make, and we almost never notice.
"We like to think our choices are driven by our personal preferences and opinions. We think we pick a certain career because we find it interesting, or buy a particular jacket because we liked the way it looked. But subtle, almost imperceptible factors have a huge effect. And by understanding the science of social influence, we can all make better decisions."
